# Google Form Final Order

This is the shorter, cleaner final order I recommend for your existing `Web Design Client Brief`.

It keeps the strong design-brief questions, but arranges them in a way that feels smoother for the client and works better for monthly-plan qualification.

## Form Title

`Web Design Client Brief`

## Suggested Description

```text
Thank you for your enquiry.

This brief helps Teodor Web Solutions understand your business, website goals, visual direction, and support needs before recommending the best next step.

Please answer as clearly as possible.
```

## Premium Description Options

### Option 1. Clear and professional

```text
Thank you for your enquiry.

This client brief helps Teodor Web Solutions understand your business, website goals, visual direction, and support needs before recommending the most suitable next step.

Please answer as clearly as possible.
```

### Option 2. More premium and consultative

```text
Thank you for your interest in working with Teodor Web Solutions.

This brief is used to understand your business, your website goals, the kind of presentation you want to create, and the level of support your project may require.

The more clearly you answer, the easier it is to recommend the right structure and prepare a more accurate next step.
```

### Option 3. Best for monthly-plan leads

```text
Thank you for your enquiry.

This brief helps Teodor Web Solutions understand your business, website goals, visual direction, and ongoing support needs before recommending whether a once-off build or a monthly website arrangement is the better fit.

Please answer as clearly as possible.
```

## Final Question Order

### 1. What is your business name?

- Type: `Short answer`
- Required: `Yes`

### 2. What does your business do?

- Type: `Paragraph`
- Required: `Yes`

### 3. Who is your target audience / ideal customer?

- Type: `Paragraph`
- Required: `Yes`

### 4. Do you have an existing website or social media links?

- Type: `Short answer` or `Paragraph`

- Type: `Multiple choice`
- Required: `Yes`

## Remove This Question

- `Number of revision rounds included`

Reason:

- it is better handled in the quote, not in the brief

## Best Flow

This order works well because it moves from:

1. business basics
2. website goals
3. pages and features
4. visual direction
5. technical setup
6. support and monthly-plan fit
7. budget and timing
