# Google Form Review

This review is based on the actual Google Form question list you provided.

## Goal

The Google Form should help qualify monthly-plan enquiries before you quote.

It should not feel like a huge admin burden, but it should collect enough information to decide:

- whether the project fits a monthly plan
- how complex the site is likely to be
- what support level the client may expect

## What Is Already Strong

Your form already does a lot well:

- it captures business context
- it asks about target audience
- it asks about brand assets
- it asks about design direction
- it asks about pages and features
- it asks about platform preference
- it asks about budget and timeline

That makes it a strong general web design brief.

## Where It Needs Improvement For Monthly-Plan Qualification

Right now, the form is strong as a design brief, but it is slightly weaker as a monthly-plan qualification form.

The main gap is that it does not clearly ask:

- why the client wants a monthly plan
- whether they expect ongoing support after launch
- what type of ongoing support they expect

Those questions matter because a monthly-plan lead is not only asking for a website. They are also asking for a different commercial structure.

## Questions You Already Have

These are useful and should stay:

1. `What is your business name?`
2. `What does your business do?`
3. `Who is your target audience / ideal customer?`
4. `Do you already have a logo, brand colors, or style guide?`
5. `Upload your brand materials`
6. `Do you have an existing website or social media links?`
7. `What is the main purpose of this website?`
8. `What actions do you want visitors to take on your site?`
9. `What problem are you hoping this website will solve?`
10. `Describe the look and feel you want for your website`
11. `List 2-3 websites you like the style of`
12. `Do you have preferred colors or fonts?`
13. `Are there any designs, colors, or styles you dislike?`
14. `Which pages do you need?`
15. `Do you have the text and images ready?`
16. `Do you want to include any of the following features?`
17. `Do you already have a domain and hosting?`
18. `Will I maintain the site after launch or will you handle updates?`
19. `Do you want a specific platform for the website?`
20. `Estimated budget range`
21. `Desired completion date`

## Recommended Changes

### 1. Add a monthly-plan intent question

Add:

- `Why are you interested in a monthly website plan?`

Type:

- `Paragraph`

Why:

- this helps you understand whether they want lower upfront cost, ongoing support, or both

### 2. Add an ongoing support expectation question

Add:

- `Do you expect ongoing support after launch?`

Type:

- `Multiple choice`

Options:

- Yes
- Maybe
- No

Why:

- this makes the monthly-plan fit much easier to judge

### 3. Add a support type question

Add:

- `What kind of ongoing support do you expect?`

Type:

- `Checkboxes`

Options:

- Technical updates
- Content edits
- Design improvements
- Landing page additions
- Advice and guidance
- Not sure yet

Why:

- this separates real support expectations from vague interest

### 4. Improve the maintenance question wording

Current question:

- `Will I maintain the site after launch or will you handle updates?`

Suggested rewrite:

- `After launch, who do you expect to handle updates and ongoing changes?`

Options:

- Teodor Dev will handle updates
- I will handle updates internally
- Not sure yet

Why:

- it reads more clearly and more professionally

### 5. Fix the platform spelling

Current option:

- `WordPres`

Change to:

- `WordPress`

### 6. Remove or rethink the revision question

Current question:

- `Number of revision rounds included`

This feels more like an internal quoting decision than a client-brief question.

Recommendation:

- remove it from the Google Form

Why:

- clients often do not know what to answer
- it sounds contractual too early
- it is better defined in your quote or agreement

### 7. Improve the `Which pages do you need?` list

Current option:

- `Option 7`

This should either be renamed or removed.

Suggested replacement:

- `Testimonials / Reviews`

or remove it completely.

## Best Final Structure

I think your Google Form should stay as a `Web Design Client Brief`, but with 3 monthly-plan qualification questions added.

That gives you the best of both:

- strong design brief detail
- better commercial qualification for monthly-plan leads

## Recommended Additions Summary

- `Why are you interested in a monthly website plan?`
- `Do you expect ongoing support after launch?`
- `What kind of ongoing support do you expect?`

## Good Form Design Advice

Keep the form as a qualification tool, not a contract.

That means:

- keep questions practical
- remove anything too legal or too pricing-specific too early
- gather enough detail to quote confidently later

## My Honest View

Your form is already quite good.

It mainly needs refinement, not rebuilding.

The biggest improvement is adding support and monthly-plan intent questions, because those are the missing pieces that help you decide whether a monthly arrangement is actually a good fit.

## Best Use In Your Workflow

Use the Google Form only after:

1. the lead selects `Monthly plan`
2. the lead is sent to the qualification step from the thank-you page or your follow-up email

That keeps the main website enquiry form short and easier to submit.
